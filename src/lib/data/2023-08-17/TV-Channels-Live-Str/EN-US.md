 CPL 2023 will commence with defending champions Jamaica Tallawahs taking on St Lucia Kings at the Darren Sammy Stadium on August 16. The tournament will run for over a month: the final is scheduled on September 25 at the Providence Stadium in Guyana.

 The Tallawahs will be led by Brandon King this season. Rovman Powell, who had led them to title glory in the previous edition was traded to Barbados Royals for Hayden Walsh.

 Powell will now take over from David Miller as the captain of the Royals. The other team with a new captain this this season is St Kitts &amp; Nevis Patriots, who will be led by Evin Lewis instead of Dwayne Bravo, who has switched to Trinbago Knight Riders.

 On single-match days, matches will start at 7 pm local time, which translates to the odd-viewing hours of 4:30 am in India. The double-header days, however, will have one match starting at 10 am local time, which is 7:30 pm India time, and the other match starting at the usual 7 pm local time.

 In India, viewers can watch CPL 2023 on FanCode. Willow TV will broadcast CPL 2023 in the United States of America.

 In the UK, BT Sport will broadcast and live-stream CPL 2023. Viewers in Australia and New Zealand can catch live action from CPL 2023 on Fox Sports and Sky Sports NZ respectively.
