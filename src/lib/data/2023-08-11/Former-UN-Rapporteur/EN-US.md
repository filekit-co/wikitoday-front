<div class="article-body">
Former UN Special Rapporteur on the situation of human rights in North Korea, Tomás Ojea Quintana, welcomed the plan for an open discussion at the Security Council, noting the gravity of the situation. However, he pointed out that despite the important symbolic significance, it will take time to achieve substantive results. Quintana called it a &#39;very good sign&#39; that the United States, South Korea, Japan, and others have called for an open session of the Security Council to discuss human rights in North Korea.


</div>

<div class="article-body">
He emphasized the importance of raising awareness at the international level about the human rights situation in North Korea. Quintana highlighted the issues of food shortages and the lack of information on the humanitarian situation in the country. He expressed concern about allegations of people starving in certain areas, calling it a serious concern.

 The North Korean Freedom Coalition also welcomed the possibility of a Security Council meeting, noting the pressing issues of North Korean defectors in China and the continuing deprivation and hunger in North Korea. Experts, however, believe that the meeting is unlikely to lead to immediate actions to improve human rights, citing the challenges posed by China and Russia. They suggested that a joint statement from member states supporting human rights in North Korea might be the possible outcome of the meeting.
